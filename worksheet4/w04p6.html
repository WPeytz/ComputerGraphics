Part 6 – Written Answers

  1. Flat vs. Gouraud vs. Phong shading:
    Flat shading computes one normal per polygon → same lighting across the face (faceted look).
    Gouraud shading computes lighting per vertex and interpolates colors across the surface → highlights may be missed if not at a vertex.
    Phong shading computes lighting per fragment using interpolated normals → best for highlights.
    Based on results in Parts 4 and 5, Phong shading is better for simulating highlights.
  2. Directional vs. Point light:
    Directional light has only a direction (like sunlight), no attenuation with distance.
    Point light emits in all directions from a position, intensity decreases with distance (1/r²).
  3. Eye position influence:
    Yes. The eye vector affects the specular reflection. Moving the eye changes the highlight position and intensity.
  4. Specular term = (0,0,0):
    Removes all specular highlights → object looks matte/dull, only diffuse + ambient lighting remain.
  5. Increasing shininess exponent (α):
    Higher α → sharper, narrower highlights (glossy).
    Lower α → broader, softer highlights (matte).
  6. Coordinate space:
    Lighting is usually computed in view (eye) space, where the camera is at the origin and all vectors are transformed accordingly.
