Texture Filtering Modes and Their Effects

Magnification (magFilter): When a texture is viewed larger than its original size.

  • Nearest: Blocky, pixelated appearance. Fast but low quality.
  • Linear: Smooth interpolation between pixels. Better quality but slightly slower.

Minification (minFilter): When a texture is viewed smaller than its original size.

  • Nearest: Samples single texel. Can cause aliasing and flickering.
  • Linear: Interpolates between 4 texels. Better but still has issues at distance.
  • Mipmap modes: Use pre-filtered smaller versions of the texture for better quality at distance.

Mipmap Filtering:

  • NEAREST_MIPMAP_NEAREST: Picks closest mipmap level, samples one texel. Fast but can show level transitions.
  • LINEAR_MIPMAP_NEAREST: Picks closest mipmap level, interpolates between texels.
  • NEAREST_MIPMAP_LINEAR: Interpolates between two mipmap levels, samples one texel per level.
  • LINEAR_MIPMAP_LINEAR (Trilinear): Best quality. Interpolates between texels AND between mipmap levels. Eliminates most aliasing.

Wrapping Modes:

  • Repeat: Texture repeats/tiles when coordinates go beyond [0,1]. Good for patterns.
  • Clamp to Edge: Coordinates are clamped to [0,1]. Edge pixels stretch beyond boundaries.

Impact on Magnification/Minification Issues: Mipmaps solve minification aliasing by providing pre-filtered textures at multiple resolutions. Without mipmaps, distant textures can shimmer and alias. Mipmaps have no effect on magnification - only mag filter matters when zooming in close.
